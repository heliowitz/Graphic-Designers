Paul Rand.
New Haven.
1956.

> About > Works


Rand's IBM logo was his most definitive work. Using a simple striped technique, the branding was eventually used for all packaging, marketing and communications for over 40 years.

His IBM logo also exemplifies the core of his design philosophy. While some have criticized it to be too simplistic and unoriginal, Rand counters that logos "do not need to be esoteric or original to be exciting".

Rand believed that frilly designs were the first to be scrapped, and had no ability to stand the test of time. He argued that simplicity and minimalism were crucial to building a brand identity that could be shaped without degrading as decades passed.

The strongest supports for Rand's beliefs are in his own logos, many of which are still used to this day.
