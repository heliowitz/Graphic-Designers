Milton Glaser is one of the most respected graphic designers in the United States. Shortly after graduating from Cooper Union, Glaser co-founded PushPin Studios.

Glaser eventually left PushPin to found New York Magazine with Clay Felkner. There, he was president and design director until 1977.

By the early 1980s, Glaser's artistic influence had global reach. Teaming up with Walter Bernard in 1983, Glaser helped to form WBMG, a publication design firm which went on to design over 50 magazines, newspapers and periodicals around the world.

Glaser also went on to found his own personal studio, where he designed logos, posters, signage and brochures for companies including FedEx, American Airlines, DC Comics, and more.
