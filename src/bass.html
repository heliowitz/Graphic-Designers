About Saul Bass

Saul Bass was an American graphic designer and Academy Award winning filmmaker. He was best known for his ability to design striking title sequences, posters, and logos.

Bass started his Hollywood career by designing print advertisements for movies. His earliest work included "Champion", "Death of a Salesman", and "The Moon is Blue". His work was highly praised, and it prompted producers to commission Bass for title sequences and closing credits as well.

Up to that point, title sequences and closing credits were largely overlooked, and thus were often bland and simple. Bass recognized the creative potential immediately.

Bass became famous in the industry after creating the title sequence for "The Man with the Golden Arm", which featured an image of an heroin-addict's arm. Eventually, Bass was creating work for Hitchcock, Preminger, Wilder, Kubrick and Scorsese.

Although most known for movie posters and title sequences, Bass also made some of the most recognized logos in America, including the Bell System logo and the AT&T logo.
