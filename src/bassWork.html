Saul
Bass.
LA.
1969.

> About > Works


Saul Bass used a very distinct and recognizable style in both his movie posters and title sequences. Like his poster for "Anatomy of a Murder", his work featured a single object or focus-piece (usually silhouetted in black). Behind it, Bass used a single colour background, usually a mix of reds, oranges and yellows.

His posters achieved a stark, barren effect that was perfect for the directors he worked with. Moreover, he used an artistic style that mimicked paper cut-outs or rough sketches.

Bass often described his posters as "a simple, visual phrase that tells you what the picture is all about and evokes the essence of the story".

Ultimately, Bass brought a visual boldness to the world of Hollywood. The barren simplicity of his projects, combined with his evocative colour schemes, allowed for him to work with some of the most revered directors of all time.
