Jacqueline Casey.
Boston.
1972.

> About > Works


Casey's signature style of poster design is shown here, for an MIT event that featured 6 artists.

Casey understood the importance of grabbing an audience's attention. Much like this one, her posters featured bold, geometric shapes against a pure black background. The information is only shown in extremely small type.

Most graphic designers would scold Casey for this strategy. They could argue that the small type and confusing design would make students less interested. In actuality, Casey's artistic decision led to a more interesting poster, and drove more students to actually get close and read about the event.
