About Massimo Vignelli
> Name:
Massimo Vignelli
> Born:
1931, Milan
> Studio:
Unimark International, Vignelli Associates
> Known for:
"New York Subway Map", "American Airlines", "Bloomingdales"


Massimo Vignelli has one of the broadest of portfolios of any famous designer. Starting out in Milan, he quickly moved to New York to help found Unimark International. It quickly became one of the largest design firms in the world.

Vignelli created some of his most notable work during his time at Unimark, including the American Airlines logo, as well as the New York City Subway Map.

Eventually, Vignelli decided to leave Unimark. He noticed that as their clientele increased, marketing forces pressured the design team to dilute their creative autonomy. Vignelli, strong-headed about his Modernist artistic ideal, decided to start Vignelli Associates.

His new company quickly picked up clients such as IBM, Knoll, Bloomingdale's and American Airlines. Eventually, Vignelli's designs saturated New York. Michael Beirut, a former employee, wrote that: "I rode in a subway with Vignelli-designed signage, shared the sidewalk with Vignelli-designed shopping bags, and walked by a church with Vignelli-designed pipe organs."
