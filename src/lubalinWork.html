Herb Lubalin.
NYC.
1962.

> About > Works


Herb Lubalin developed the ITC Avant Garde Gothic font family. Initially used in Avant Garde magazine, the markup was designed with his partner Tom Carnase.

The typeface was initially just the logo of their magazine, Avant Garde. However, impressed with their own work, Lubalin and Carnase decided to implement it into an entire font type. This proved to be difficult, given the font's incredibly small letter spacing. However, it was this, combined with the sharp angular lines, that gave the font it's definitive identity.

The font became quite popular, especially among designers. Unfortunately, Lubalin felt like the font was being misused, and oversaturated the type culture of the 1970s.
